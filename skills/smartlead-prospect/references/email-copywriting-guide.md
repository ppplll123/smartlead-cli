# Email Copywriting Guide for Outbound Prospecting

## Core Principles — Non-Negotiable

### 1. Targeted Outreach beats automated blasting (ALWAYS)
Personalize (even a little): Reference something specific — the job they're hiring for, a piece of tech they use, a recent company mention, something from their LinkedIn. This shows you're not just spamming.

Focus on their potential need: Connect what you offer to why they specifically might care right now.

### 2. Multi-Channel Approach
Don't just live in email. People get a ton of emails.
Reach out on 2-3 platforms per person:
- Email: workhorse for initial outreach and follow-ups
- LinkedIn: connection requests, InMail, messages to connections
- Phone: actually call them — especially good for high-value targets
- Social media (FB, IG, WhatsApp): if available

### 3. Follow up 3-4x on ALL platforms
Keep the gap between messages/emails to 1-2 days to maximize output. Most replies come from follow-ups, not first touch.

### 4. Key parts of a good outreach message
- **Be natural and authentic** — no corporate or GPT jargon
- **Keep it short and simple** — straight to the point
- **Use their industry language** — speak like an insider, not a marketer
- If messages are opened but "No Reply" → review and adjust scripting (each vertical is different)

## Subject Lines That Work

| Subject Line | When to Use |
|---|---|
| `[Their Company] <> [Your Company]` | Partnership framing |
| `We pay for your marketing - you get leads` | Value-first |
| `Can we send you more leads?` | Direct |
| `New Customers - Partnership Inquiry` | Formal |
| `Partnership Proposal - Channeling Prospects to Your Firm` | Professional |
| `Quality Leads to [VERTICAL] - Partnership Opportunity` | Vertical-specific |
| `Partnership Opportunity` | Simple |

From our own data: `"{{first_name}} - partnership request | {{location}}"` = best reply rate (1.16%)

## Cold Intro Emails — 10 Proven Templates

### Copy 1 — LinkedIn Reference (Short)
> Hi {{first_name}},
> I found you on LinkedIn.
> Are you currently generating leads in-house or have 3rd party vendors?
> I own a [vertical brand] and wanted to check if there is an opportunity for partnership.
> We can supply anywhere from 50-200 qualified leads per day.

### Copy 3 — Partnership + Social Proof
> Hi {{first_name}},
> I am reaching out on behalf of [your brand] and looking for someone who is in charge of partnerships at your firm.
> We own a portfolio of brands in the [vertical] and we would like to provide real-time leads/appointments.
> Already working with [notable names they know] and similar.
> What's the primary product you currently focus on?

### Copy 4 — Partnership Inquiry (Our Best Performer)
> Hi {{first_name}},
> Looking for someone who is responsible for partnerships at your company.
> We would like to establish a lead generation partnership, as we have prospects we would like to refer.
> For context, we own a portfolio of brands where we spend a significant budget on marketing to acquire consumers interested in [vertical product].
> Currently have more leads we need to distribute and are looking for new partners. We recently partnered with [notable names].

### Copy 6 — Portfolio Angle + CTA
> Hi {{first_name}},
> I'm reaching out as we would be interested in a potential partnership with your company.
> We own a portfolio of high-traffic brands where we educate consumers on different [vertical] products.
> If they are interested in any product, we collect all their contact info, verify it and forward it to partners like you.
> Our other partners are [notable names] and many more.
> Would you be open to a discussion?

### Copy 7 — Conversion Rate Proof
> Hi {{first_name}},
> I own a [vertical brand] and wanted to check if there is an opportunity for partnership with your company.
> I would like to send you qualified leads (we see XX% conversion rate on average in your states)
> Here you can see the customer flow: [link to quiz]
> Please let me know if we can integrate you and start sending leads.

### Copy 8 — "We Pay for Your Marketing"
> Hi {{first_name}},
> I own a [vertical brand] and wanted to check if there is an opportunity for partnership with your company.
> We would like to pay for your marketing, generate qualified leads and then forward the ones that meet your criteria directly to you.
> Here you can see the customer flow: [link to quiz]
> Please let me know if we can integrate you and start sending leads.

### Copy 9 — Higher Reply Rate (Short + One-Word CTA)
> Hi {{first_name}},
> We're looking for an additional partner for our [vertical] brand.
> We can supply qualified leads at $X cost per [conversion event].
> Is [company name] currently working with any lead vendors or do you do marketing internally?
> One-word reply works: Yes/No/More info.
> Appreciate your 10 seconds.

### Copy 10 — Higher Reply Rate (Flattery + Specifics)
> Hi {{first_name}},
> I follow your company for some time and also seeing your [ads/content] quite frequently. We would be interested in some sort of partnership (if you would be open to it) where we supply you pre-qualified leads.
> We currently generate XX-XXX leads per day (OTP verified that go through XX-step questionnaire). Typical cost per [conversion] is $XYZ.
> Do you have capacity for more leads?

## Follow-Up Emails — 4 Proven Templates

### Follow-Up 1 — In-Person Meeting Angle
> I should be in [their city] next week.
> Can we connect? Or here's my calendar for online meeting if that's easier. [link]

### Follow-Up 2 — Urgency + Calendar
> {{first_name}}, what time would be best to call you?
> I would like to start sending you leads in the next 2 weeks.
> Here's my calendar if that's easier. [link]

### Follow-Up 3 — Restate Value Prop
> We will pay for your marketing, generate qualified leads and you only pay us for the leads you receive and meet your criteria.
> I can start sending you the leads within 7-days, just need more info on what type of customers you prefer.
> Please email me back or here's my calendar if that's easier. [link]

### Follow-Up 4 — Ultra Short (Integration Question)
> Would you prefer to get leads to CRM or just notification to your team email/phone?

## Whale Client Strategy

### Finding Whales
- Target companies spending $50K+/mo on marketing in your vertical
- Look for companies already using lead vendors (they understand the model)
- Check if they're running paid ads (Google, Facebook) — if yes, they have budget
- Target VP/Director of Marketing or Business Development, not junior staff

### Personalization for Whales
- Reference their specific ad campaigns or content you've seen
- Mention their competitors by name (shows you know the space)
- Reference recent news (new office, acquisition, hiring)
- Use LinkedIn to find warm intros through mutual connections

### Whale-Specific Templates

**The "I See Your Ads" Approach:**
> {{first_name}}, I've been seeing [company] ads on [platform] quite a bit lately — particularly the [specific campaign/offer].
> We generate [XX] verified [vertical] leads per day and currently work with [competitor names].
> Would it make sense to explore adding us as a supplemental source? Even 50 leads/week to test.

**The "Competitor Name-Drop" Approach:**
> {{first_name}}, we currently supply leads to [their competitor] and are looking to expand.
> Given [their company]'s presence in [location/vertical], there could be a fit.
> Would you be open to a quick conversation about what that partnership might look like?

**The "Revenue Angle" for C-Suite:**
> {{first_name}}, [their company] appears to be in a growth phase based on [specific evidence].
> We help companies like yours add $X-Y in revenue by supplying pre-qualified [vertical] leads at [cost model].
> Our partners typically see [X]% conversion. Worth a 10-minute call?

## Outbound Interaction Sequence (Full Cadence)

1. **LinkedIn** — Send connection request + personalized note
2. **Email** — Send intro email (use templates above)
3. **Social media** — FB Page DM, IG DM, WhatsApp if available
4. **Call** — Call the prospect or company directly
5. **Repeat** — Follow up 3-4x across ALL channels

Gap between touches: 1-2 days
Minimum companies to contact: 250+ before changing strategy

## What NOT to Do

- NO automated mass blasting without personalization
- NO corporate jargon or GPT-sounding copy
- NO long emails — if it's more than 5 sentences for first touch, it's too long
- NO sending without email verification — bounces destroy reputation
- NO giving up after 1 email — most replies come from follow-ups 2-4
- NO using the same script for every vertical — insurance ≠ solar ≠ legal
- NO ignoring the thread context when following up (read the conversation first)
